Meet Your Tutor

Abram Caroline, age 13

Abram is currently our only tutor at Rise Math Tutoring — and math has been his thing for as long as he can remember. He's the kid who finishes the assignment early and then goes around helping classmates who are stuck, not because he has to, but because he genuinely likes explaining how a problem clicks into place.

Right now, Abram is enrolled in a private, self-paced math program at his school, where he's working through multiple high-school-level math classes simultaneously — well ahead of a typical 13-year-old's course load. That self-paced structure has also made him especially good at breaking big concepts into small, manageable steps, since that's exactly how he's learned to teach himself.

Because he's close in age to many of his students, Abram remembers what it's like to get stuck on a concept — and he's patient about explaining it more than one way until it makes sense.

Backed By Research

Peer tutoring isn't just a nice idea — it's well studied

Decades of education research point to the same conclusion: students learning from a peer, rather than only from an adult instructor, tend to see real gains in both performance and confidence.

0%

Of math peer-tutoring programs showed gains

A review pooling 50 independent studies on peer tutoring in math, spanning early childhood through college, found that the large majority of programs had a measurable positive effect on academic performance.

Alegre-Ansuategui et al., Eurasia J. Math., Science & Tech. Ed.
0

Effect size for tutors, not just tutees

A meta-analysis of peer tutoring found that the tutor's own understanding improves meaningfully through the act of teaching — explaining a concept out loud deepens mastery of it.

Leung, Educational Psychology in Practice
0

Students studied across STEM peer tutoring

A recent meta-analysis covering thousands of students across STEM subjects found a strong overall effect of peer tutoring on academic achievement.

ScienceDirect, 2025 STEM meta-analysis

Research also suggests high-achieving tutors tend to produce stronger outcomes for tutees — one of the reasons a strong personal grasp of the material matters as much as teaching patience.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How long are sessions?

Sessions are typically 60 minutes, though shorter sessions can be arranged for younger students.

What ages do you tutor?

I work with students from elementary school through high school, covering everything from basic arithmetic to Algebra and Geometry.

Online or in person?

Both are available. Online sessions use a shared virtual whiteboard; in-person sessions can be arranged locally.

What if my child is behind?

That's exactly what the diagnostic assessment is for. We'll identify gaps first and build a plan that starts at the right level, not just the current grade level.

How do payments work?

Payment details are confirmed after your free consultation, with options to pay per session or purchase a discounted package upfront.
